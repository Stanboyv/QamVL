Aim

To study Quadrature Amplitude Modulation (QAM) and visualize its constellation diagram, signal waveforms, and bit error rate using interactive simulations.

Theory

Quadrature Amplitude Modulation (QAM) combines amplitude modulation of two carriers in quadrature (90° phase difference). It is widely used in digital communication systems such as Wi-Fi, LTE, and cable modems.

  • Constellation Diagram: Represents symbols as points in the I-Q plane.
  • Bit Mapping: Each symbol encodes multiple bits depending on modulation order (e.g., 16-QAM encodes 4 bits per symbol).
  • Noise Impact: Higher-order QAM is more bandwidth-efficient but more susceptible to noise.

Results & Observations

The simulation shows how noise affects symbol placement in the constellation diagram and the resulting bit error rate (BER). Below are detailed observations for different QAM orders:

4-QAM (QPSK)

  • Constellation has 4 points arranged in quadrature.
  • Very robust to noise, suitable for low SNR channels.
  • Working range: 6–10 dB SNR.
  • Safest range: ≥10 dB ensures reliable communication with very low BER.
  • Used in satellite links and very noisy channels where reliability is critical.

16-QAM

  • Constellation has 16 points, encoding 4 bits per symbol.
  • Balances bandwidth efficiency with moderate robustness.
  • Working range: 15–20 dB SNR.
  • Safest range: ≥18 dB for stable performance.
  • Commonly used in Wi-Fi and cable modems for medium‑quality channels.

64-QAM

  • Constellation has 64 points, encoding 6 bits per symbol.
  • High bandwidth efficiency but sensitive to noise.
  • Working range: 25–30 dB SNR.
  • Safest range: ≥28 dB for reliable transmission.
  • Widely used in LTE and advanced broadband systems where channel quality is good.

256-QAM

  • Constellation has 256 points, encoding 8 bits per symbol.
  • Extremely bandwidth‑efficient but highly vulnerable to noise.
  • Working range: 33–36 dB SNR.
  • Safest range: ≥35 dB to minimize BER.
  • Used in modern fiber and 5G systems where channel conditions are excellent.
