Quadrature Amplitude Modulation (QAM)

Aim Theory Procedure Pretest Simulation Posttest Results

Aim

To study and simulate Quadrature Amplitude Modulation (QAM) and analyze its constellation diagram, time-domain waveform, and Bit Error Rate (BER) performance under noise.

Theory

Quadrature Amplitude Modulation (QAM) is a digital modulation technique that conveys data by changing the amplitude of two carrier waves that are out of phase by 90 degrees.

In M-QAM, each symbol represents log2(M) bits. Higher-order QAM improves bandwidth efficiency but requires higher SNR for reliable communication.

Mathematical Representation:

s(t) = I(t)cos(2πfct) − Q(t)sin(2πfct)

Procedure

  1. Select the modulation order (M-QAM).
  2. Generate random binary data.
  3. Map bits to QAM symbols.
  4. Add AWGN noise to the signal.
  5. Plot constellation and waveform.
  6. Demodulate received symbols.
  7. Compute Bit Error Rate (BER).
